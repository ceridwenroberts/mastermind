How to play

The codebreaker (you) has 10 attempts at breaking a randomly selected code consisting of a series of 4 coloured pegs. Each of the 6 colours may occur a maximum of two 2 times.

Hints

After each attempt you get feedback through red and white hintpegs. A red peg means that there is a peg of the right colour in right place. White means that the pegcolour is in the code, but in the wrong place. The placement of these hints do not indicate which numer it refers to. Even though a red peg means that there is a peg of the right colour in the right place, there will be no hint for an other occurece of the same color in the wrong place.

Scoring

Your score is tallied at the end of each round.
If you break the code
you get one point for each empty row.
If you do not break the code
and you use all of your 10 guesses without breaking the code, you loose the game.
